About

I’m especially interested in the point where math, software, and visual intuition overlap. A good chunk of my work is about making hidden structure visible: not just computing something correctly, but building a way to look at it, rotate it, poke at it, and understand it.

I’m drawn to projects where the explanation is part of the product. That might mean a 3D visualization of dimensional analysis, a graphics engine built around geometric algebra, or a machine learning tool that makes its own internal logic easier to grasp.

In other words: I like software that thinks out loud.

Focus Machine learning, technical visualization, and interactive mathematical software.
Style Friendly, curious, and explanation-first.

RotorVision

Geometric algebra + 3D graphics

A small 3D graphics engine that uses geometric-algebra rotors instead of quaternions to drive every rotation in the scene. It’s part graphics demo, part mathematical experiment, and part attempt to make a beautiful abstract idea feel concrete in code.

The demo includes a rotor-spun torus knot, a rotor-driven camera rig, and a live HUD showing the current rotor components. It’s a project about rendering, yes — but also about choosing the right language for rotation.

physical metric space

Dimensional analysis + interactive visualization

An interactive visualization of dimensional analysis in L/M/T exponent space, where physical quantities become points in a navigable 3D geometry. Instead of treating units and dimensions as static symbols, this project turns them into a space you can explore.

The result is a more visual way of thinking about physical structure: clusters, equivalences, and relationships become easier to notice when they live in a world instead of a table.
