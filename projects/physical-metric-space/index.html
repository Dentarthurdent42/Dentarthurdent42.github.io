Dimensional Analysis · L M T Exponent Space

Welcome, Behrooz. What you are about to explore is, in a precise sense, a semantic embedding — except the embedding space predates machine learning by two centuries, and the geometry is not learned but exact.

Every physical quantity — force, energy, pressure, viscosity — can be expressed as a product of powers of three base dimensions: Length (L), Mass (M), and Time (T).

The structure that emerges is not decorative. Quantities sharing a point are dimensionally identical. Nearby regions often encode meaningful structural relationships.

For someone working at the intersection of formula search and mathematical information retrieval, dimensional exponents are a compact, language-independent coordinate system.

The origin — the single point at (0, 0, 0) — is the home of all dimensionless quantities: strain, Mach number, Reynolds number, angles.
